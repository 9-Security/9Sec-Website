PRIVACY_POLICY

> Effective date: 2026-06-25  ·  Last updated: 2026-06-25

This Privacy Policy explains how Nine-Security (“Nine-Security”, “we”, “us”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data when you visit our website, contact us, or use our consulting services and OpenXDR / MDR platform (the “Services”). We process personal data in accordance with applicable law, including Taiwan’s Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA).

1. Data We Collect

Account & contact data — name, work email, phone, organization, and the credentials you use to sign in to the console.

Security telemetry — from systems you enroll, the endpoint agent and platform collect security-relevant events. This is used solely to detect and respond to threats and is governed by your deployment authorization. It includes:

  • Endpoint & host identity — hostname, operating-system version, a machine identifier, agent version, local IP address(es), last reboot, and the logged-on user.
  • Process activity — process names, command-line arguments, parent/child relationships, executable paths, file hashes (SHA-256), code-signing status, and the run-as account and integrity level. (Command lines and account names can contain personal data.)
  • File activity — creation, rename, and deletion of executables and of files in sensitive or auto-start locations, plus events indicating a process opened a credential store. We do not collect the contents of your files in the course of monitoring.
  • Registry activity — changes and deletions to auto-start, service, and security-relevant registry keys.
  • Network activity — outbound connection destinations (IP and port), DNS hostnames queried, and local listening sockets.
  • Authentication & account activity — Windows logon events (success and failure) including the account name, logon type, and source IP address; account and group-membership changes; service installations; and security-log clearing.
  • Script activity — the de-obfuscated content of scripts inspected by the Antimalware Scan Interface (AMSI) where a detection rule applies.
  • Removable media — mounting of USB / removable volumes (drive letter, volume label, filesystem).
  • Detection findings — rule matches, severity, and MITRE ATT&CK mappings.

Files submitted for analysis — file contents are collected only when you or an authorized operator explicitly submit a file (or run an investigative “deep scan”); the agent does not perform general content inspection of your documents or bulk file exfiltration.

Usage data — log, device, and analytics data about how the Services are used. Communications — information you provide when you contact us or request a trial.

2. How We Use Data

  • to provide, operate, secure, and improve the Services;
  • to detect, investigate, and respond to security threats on enrolled systems;
  • to authenticate users and administer accounts and subscriptions;
  • to respond to inquiries and provide support;
  • to comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

3. Legal Bases

Where required, we process personal data on the basis of contract performance, our legitimate interests in operating and securing the Services, your consent (which you may withdraw), and compliance with legal obligations.

4. Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell personal data. We share it only with: service providers and sub-processors who process data on our behalf under appropriate safeguards; your own authorized administrators within your tenant; and authorities where required by law or to protect rights and safety. Each customer’s data is logically isolated within a per-tenant datalake.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as needed to provide the Services and for legitimate business or legal purposes. Security telemetry is retained according to your subscription’s retention settings; expired or terminated trial tenants are purged on a scheduled basis.

6. Security

We apply technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including encryption in transit, per-tenant isolation, signed and authenticated agent enrollment, access controls, and audit logging. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. International Transfers

Your data may be processed in locations outside your country. Where it is, we use appropriate safeguards consistent with applicable law.

8. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, object to certain processing, or obtain a copy of it. To exercise these rights, contact privacy@nine-security.com. If you are an end user of a customer’s tenant, please direct requests to that customer (the data controller); we will assist them as a processor.

9. Cookies

Our website and console use strictly necessary cookies/local storage (for example, to maintain your session and preferences). We do not use them for advertising.

10. Children’s Privacy

The Services are intended for organizations and business users and are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be indicated by updating the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, by additional notice.

12. Contact

Privacy questions or requests: privacy@nine-security.com.

Note: This document is a general template provided for transparency and is not legal advice. We recommend reviewing it with qualified legal counsel and tailoring it to your specific data practices and jurisdictions before relying on it.
